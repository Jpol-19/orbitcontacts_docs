Child Safety and Age-Appropriate Design Standards

1. Introduction

At Orbit ("we," "our," or "us"), the safety of all users, particularly children, is our highest priority. This Child Safety and Age-Appropriate Design Standards (CSAE) document outlines our policies and commitments to prevent, detect, and respond to Child Sexual Abuse and Exploitation (CSAE) content, including Child Sexual Abuse Material (CSAM).

Orbit is a personal contact management application that helps users organize contacts, remember important dates, and send cards and gifts. This policy applies to all users and all content shared through our platform.

Zero Tolerance Policy

Orbit has a strict zero-tolerance policy for Child Sexual Abuse and Exploitation (CSAE) and Child Sexual Abuse Material (CSAM).

Any content, behavior, or activity that exploits, endangers, or harms children is strictly prohibited and will result in immediate account termination, content removal, and reporting to appropriate law enforcement agencies and the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC).

2. Prohibited Content and Conduct

2.1 Strictly Prohibited Content

The following content and conduct are absolutely prohibited on Orbit:

  • Child Sexual Abuse Material (CSAM): Any visual depiction, description, or material involving the sexual exploitation or abuse of minors
  • Sexualization of Minors: Any content that sexualizes or depicts minors in a sexually suggestive manner
  • Exploitation Content: Any content that facilitates, advertises, or promotes the exploitation or endangerment of children
  • Inappropriate Images: Any images of minors uploaded to the platform that violate these standards

2.2 Age Requirements

Users must be at least 13 years of age to create an Orbit account. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13.

3. App Design and Privacy Features

3.1 Privacy-First Design

Orbit is designed with safety and privacy as core principles:

  • Private by Default: All user data, including contacts and images, is private to the individual user
  • No Public Profiles: Orbit does not feature public user profiles or social discovery mechanisms
  • No User-to-User Messaging: The app does not facilitate direct communication between users
  • Personal Contact Management: The app is designed for managing existing personal contacts, not for meeting new people
  • Secure Authentication: Account creation requires verified email addresses through trusted identity providers (Google Sign-In, Sign in with Apple)
  • Secure Storage: All user data is stored securely using Firebase services with access controls

3.2 Limited Data Collection

Orbit collects only the data necessary to provide our contact management services:

  • User account information (email, name)
  • Contact information you choose to import and store
  • Profile pictures and images you upload
  • Payment information (processed securely through Stripe for card orders)

We do not collect or share data with third parties for advertising purposes.

4. Reporting Mechanisms

4.1 How to Report Child Safety Concerns

Report Suspicious Content or Behavior

If you encounter content or behavior that violates our child safety policies, please report it immediately via email:

Report Child Safety Concern

What to include in your report:

  • Detailed description of the content or behavior
  • Account information (if known)
  • Date and time of the incident
  • Any relevant information (do not forward CSAM)

All reports are reviewed promptly and treated with strict confidentiality.

4.2 Our Response Process

When we receive a child safety report, we will:

  1. Review: Promptly review all reports received
  2. Investigate: Assess the reported content or behavior against our policies
  3. Take Action: If content violates our policies:
    • Immediately remove violating content
    • Suspend or permanently terminate involved accounts
    • Preserve evidence as required by law
    • Report to NCMEC and law enforcement as legally required

4.3 External Reporting

Users can also report child exploitation directly to:

  • National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC): www.cybertipline.org or call 1-800-THE-LOST (1-800-843-5678)
  • FBI's Internet Crime Complaint Center (IC3): www.ic3.gov
  • Local Law Enforcement: Call 911 for immediate emergencies

5. Legal Compliance and Reporting Obligations

5.1 Mandatory Reporting

Orbit is committed to full compliance with all applicable child protection laws and regulations, including:

  • 18 U.S.C. ยง 2258A: Upon obtaining actual knowledge of apparent CSAM, we will file a report with NCMEC's CyberTipline as required by federal law
  • COPPA (Children's Online Privacy Protection Act): We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13
  • State and International Laws: Compliance with applicable state and international child protection regulations

5.2 NCMEC CyberTipline Reporting

If we obtain actual knowledge of apparent CSAM on our platform, we will:

  • File a report with NCMEC's CyberTipline in accordance with federal law
  • Preserve relevant evidence as required by law
  • Cooperate fully with law enforcement investigations
  • Not notify users under investigation to avoid interfering with law enforcement

5.3 Law Enforcement Cooperation

We respond to valid legal requests from law enforcement agencies and cooperate with investigations involving child safety. Law enforcement may contact us at neworbitorders@gmail.com with "LAW ENFORCEMENT REQUEST" in the subject line.

6. Enforcement Actions

6.1 Consequences for Violations

Users who violate our CSAE policies will face:

  • Immediate Content Removal: Permanent deletion of violating content
  • Account Termination: Immediate and permanent ban from the platform
  • Reporting to Authorities: Reports filed with NCMEC and law enforcement as required by law
  • No Appeals: There is no appeal process for CSAE violations

6.2 Zero Exceptions

Our CSAE policy has no exceptions:

  • Age of the user is irrelevant to enforcement
  • Private content is subject to the same standards if we become aware of violations
  • All suspected CSAM will be reported to NCMEC regardless of circumstances

7.1 For Parents and Guardians

We encourage parents and guardians to:

  • Use device-level parental controls (iOS Screen Time, Android Family Link)
